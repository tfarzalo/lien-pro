**URL:** `/texas/lien-filing-cheat-sheet/`

**Question 1: Property Type**
- "Is the Property 'Commercial' or 'Residential'?"
- Definition of residential: Property designed for residential purposes, owned by single natural person (not entity), up to 4 families

**Content Requirements:**

#### B. **Commercial Lien Filing Process**
**URL:** `/texas/lien-filing-cheat-sheet/commercial-project/`

**Question 2: Hiring Party**
- "Were You Hired by the Property Owner, General Contractor, or Subcontractor?"
- Different requirements based on relationship
- Links to detailed flows for each scenario

**Required Steps (Subcontractor/Supplier hired by GC):**

1. **Serve Pre-Lien Notice** (2nd Month 15th)
   - Must include specific information
   - Deadline: 15th day of 2nd month after first furnishing
   - Example calculations provided
   - Recipients: Property Owner AND General Contractor

2. **Serve Third Month Notice** (Optional but recommended)
   - Additional protection step
   - Deadline: 15th day of 3rd month
   - Shows continued work

3. **Serve Notice of Contractual Retainage** (If applicable)
   - Only for withheld retainage
   - Must serve 30 days before lien filing
   - Specific to contractual retainage vs. fund trapping

4. **Prepare Mechanics Lien Affidavit**
   - Detailed requirements listed
   - Statutory form must be used
   - Must include specific information:
     - Property description
     - Labor/materials provided
     - Amounts owed
     - Dates of first/last work
     - Parties involved

5. **File Mechanics Lien**
   - File in county clerk's office
   - Deadline: 15th day of 4th month after last work
   - Property Owner holds funds until lien released

6. **Serve Pre-Lien Filing Payment Demand**
   - Strong letter before filing
   - References Prompt Payment Act
   - Attorney-drafted recommended

7. **File Lawsuit to Foreclose** (If unpaid)
   - Deadline: 2 years after lien filing or 1 year after completion
   - Can include:
     - Breach of contract claims
     - Prompt Payment Act violations
     - Attorney fees
     - Interest

#### C. **Commercial Lien - Property Owner Contract**

### 3. **Lien Laws Library**

#### **Key Articles:**

**A. "What is a Texas Construction Lien?"**
Content Required:
- Definition: Legal claim against property for unpaid labor/materials
- Available to: Contractors, Subcontractors, Architects, Engineers, Material Suppliers, Equipment Lessors
- Purpose: Protects property with lien, prevents clear title
- Benefits:
  - Creates cloud on title
  - Blocks refinancing/sale
  - Gives leverage for payment
  - Can lead to foreclosure
- Legal basis: Texas Property Code

**B. "What is a Pre-Lien Notice?"**
Content Required:
- Definition: Written notice to property owner and GC of right to file lien
- Required for: Anyone not hired by property owner
- Deadline: 15th day of 2nd month after first furnishing
- Consequence of missing: Cannot file valid lien
- Must include:
  - Description of labor/materials
  - Name/address of claimant
  - Name of person who hired claimant
  - Property address
- Inside Tips:
  1. General Contractors should send too (for strategic reasons)
  2. Use payment demand letter instead on GC disputes

**C. "Commercial vs. Residential Requirements"**
Content Required:
- Definition differences
- Deadline variations:
  - Residential: 15th day of 3rd month
  - Commercial: 15th day of 4th month
- Notice requirements differences
- Examples of each property type

### 4. **Payment Bond Claims (Public Projects)**
**URL:** `/texas/bond-claim-cheat-sheet/`

**Content Structure:**

#### **Question 1: Hiring Party**
- Hired by General Contractor vs. Subcontractor
- Different notice requirements

#### **Bond Claim Process:**

1. **Obtain Bond Information**
   - Request from GC within 10 days
   - Must provide:
     - Bond company name
     - Amount
     - Surety company address

2. **Serve Second Month Notice of Claim**
   - Deadline: 2nd month after first furnishing
   - Recipients: GC and owner

3. **Serve Third Month Notice** (Not required but recommended)
   - Strategic advantage

4. **Notice of Claim for Retainage**
   - 15th day of month following completion
   - Must include specific accounting

5. **Serve Pre-Lawsuit Payment Demand**
   - Final demand before litigation
   - Attorney-drafted recommended

6. **File Lawsuit Against Bond**
   - Deadline: Within 1 year after last furnishing
   - Or within 1 year after final completion
   - Failure to file = waiver of claim

**Inside Tips:**
- Always send courtesy informal request first
- Keep detailed records
- Send certified mail
- Consider attorney representation

### 7. **Common Mistakes Page**
**URL:** `/texas/mechanics-lien-filing-mistakes/`

**The 5 Biggest Lien Filing Mistakes:**

1. **Using Free "Fill in the Blank" Forms from Internet**
   - Problem: Not state-specific
   - Problem: Missing strategic elements
   - Problem: Generic language
   - Solution: Use attorney-drafted forms

2. **Waiting to Start Lien Process (Repeated Promise to Pay)**
   - Problem: Missing deadlines
   - Problem: Losing leverage
   - Solution: Start immediately when payment late

3. **Only Completing "Half" of the Lien Filing Process**
   - Problem: Not following through
   - Problem: Missing deadlines for lawsuit
   - Problem: Lien expires worthless
   - Solution: Complete entire process including lawsuit if needed

4. **Using Online "Document Prep" Company**
   - Problem: Not staffed by lawyers
   - Problem: Cannot give legal advice
   - Problem: No strategic advantage
   - Solution: Use lawyer or quality DIY kit

5. **Failing to Make Lien Filing a Routine in Your Business**
   - Problem: Reactive instead of proactive
   - Problem: Missing opportunities
   - Solution: Make it standard practice, not emergency response

**Key Takeaway:** 
- Best approach: Assume all clients may need lien process
- Send preliminary notices routinely
- Keep detailed records from start
- Don't wait for payment problems
